Felines :

The domestic cat is a small, typically furry, carnivoruus mammel. Cats are valued by humans for companionship and for their ability to hunt vermin. There are more than 70 breeds of cats, though differant associations proclaim differant numbers according to their standards.

Conservation status : Domesticated
*Scientific classification*
Kingdom : Animalia
Phylum : Chordata
Class : Mammalia
Order : Carnivora
Family : Felidae
Genus : Felis
Species : F. catus

Cats can suffer from a wide range of health problems, including infectious diseases, parasites, injuries, and chronic disease. Vaccinations are available for many of these diseases, and domestic cats are regularly given treatments to eliminate parasites such as worms and fleas.

Vaccination :

Most vaccination protocols recommend a series of vaccines for kittens, with vaccine boosters given at one year of age. Frequency of vaccination thereafter varies depending on the lifestyle of the individual cat, including:

  • indoor vs outdoor
  • travel plans
  • kennel/boarding plans
  • underlying disease conditions
  • other exposure risks
  • the disease and vaccine type being considered
